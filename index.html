Profile

My name is Andrea, and I was born in Italy almost 21 years ago. I'm a third year mathematics bachelor student interested in the following:
  • rings and fields theory,
  • Galois theory,
  • hyperbolic geometry,
  • algebraic combinatorics,
  • algebraic number theory,
  • Cluster algebras,
  • quiver representation theory.
Currently working on my bachelor thesis; which will be an examination of the combinatorial aspects of surface Cluster algebras, and connections to Frobenius' conjecture. I just finished working on a detailed Computer-Assisted proof report of the four color theorem; more precisely, the Dürre-Heesch D-reducibility algorithm involving 2822 unavoidable configurations.

Talks

Combinatorial aspects of surface cluster Algebras: An introduction

On February 24th 2023, I gave a quick introduction to my bachelor thesis; more precisely, combinatorial aspects of surface Cluster algebras and applications to Frobenius' conjecture. The main part of my talk was focused on explaining process of generating a Cluster algebra by using the triangulated puncture torus to construct a cluster quiver to then construct a cluster mutation that generates all Markov triples.

Finitely generated modules over principal ideal domains

Abstract: The purpose of this report is to give a detailed description about the structure and behaviour of finitely generated modules over principal ideal domains (PIDs), that are especially nice rings. This type of analysis is particularly important if we apply it to the ring of integers, \(\mathbb{Z}\), which is a PID; as it allows for a proof of the Fundamental Theorem of Finitely Generated Abelian Groups. We will describe an important concept called the Invariant Factor Form of a finitely generated module. We can then use the result as stated above and apply it in the special case of the PID \(F[x]\), i.e., the ring of polynomials in \(x\) with coefficients in the field \(F\). More precisely, when we use the Invariant Factor Decomposition of a finite dimensional vector space \(V\) over a field \(F\), i.e., \(V\) is an \(F[x]\)-module where \(x\) acts on \(V\) as a linear transformation \(T\), we obtain the Rational Canonical Form for the matrix for \(T\).

Frieze Patterns of Integers and Triangulated Polygons

Abstract: This report seeks to examine finite frieze patterns of non negative integers, introduced in the early seventies by Coxeter. There are two primary aims of this paper. Firstly, it aims to prove the bijection between triangulations of convex \(n\)-gons and frieze patterns of order \(n\) discovered by Conway and Coxeter and discuss the construction of a frieze pattern from its associated convex polygon. Next, this paper seeks to use a connection to snake graphs to prove that every such frieze pattern must contain a four or consist entirely of Fibonacci numbers.
